Before / After

How I sharpen
content

Example 01 — Generic announcement → Community-native launch copy
✗ Before — What most brands write
"We are excited to announce the launch of our new community ambassador program. Join us and help spread the word about our project. Limited spots available. Apply now to earn rewards."
✓ After — Mayor's version
We're giving power back to the community.

The DSF Ambassador Program is live — and we're looking for the builders, the degens, and the believers who want to own a piece of what's coming.

Limited spots. Real rewards.
Apply now. 👇
Why it works
"We are excited to announce" is the most skipped phrase in crypto. The rewrite opens with a power statement, then uses identity language — "builders, degens, believers" — so readers self-select and feel called in, not pushed at. "Own a piece of what's coming" taps the core crypto motivation: being early. Same message. 10x the pull.
Example 02 — Data update → Hook post (real result: 3.5K views, 2nd place win)
✗ Before — Standard market update
"Arbitrum has recorded $1.3 billion in DeFi inflows across bridges in the last 24 hours. This demonstrates strong ecosystem activity. Follow us for more DeFi updates."
✓ After — Mayor's version
Arbitrum just recorded $1.3B in inflows across DeFi and bridges in the last 24 hours.

This is everything you need to know in 2 minutes. 🎬
Why it works
The number is the hook — lead with it. The "2 minutes" promise removes friction before the click. The 🎬 signals the format instantly. This exact post hit 3.5K impressions, 96 likes, 15 RTs and won 2nd place in a content competition. Praised publicly by Keith A. Grossman for its "cinematic animation, pacing, and music."
Example 03 — Product pitch → Debate-driving collab post (amplified by MoonPay)
✗ Before — Standard product copy
"MoonPay makes it easy to buy crypto. With support for 160+ currencies and 160+ countries, MoonPay is the fastest and most trusted on-ramp. Try it today."
✓ After — Mayor's version
You might have seen @moonpay on your timeline.

But have you actually stopped to ask —
why use MoonPay when you can just buy crypto on a CEX or DEX?

The answer might change how you think about on-ramps. 👇
Why it works
Product descriptions don't go viral. Questions do. The "But have you actually stopped to ask" construction forces a pause — it makes the reader feel smart for wanting the answer. By framing MoonPay as a puzzle instead of a pitch, it earns curiosity instead of triggering ad-blindness. Result: MoonPay's own account amplified it.
My process

How I think
before I write

01
Understand the audience
I live in the communities I write for — Discord, Telegram, X. What are they saying? What do they scroll past? What makes them repost? Data + vibes, never assumptions.
02
Find the real goal
Most briefs say "awareness." I dig: is the goal FOMO? Trust? Sign-ups? Debate? The right goal reshapes the hook, the CTA, and the entire format of the piece.
03
Write for the platform
A Telegram message ≠ an X thread ≠ a Discord post. I match tone and structure to context. Platform-native writing performs. Repurposed copy dies quietly.
04
Measure & adapt
Impressions, growth, engagement rate — I track and feed it back into the next piece. I design visuals myself (Canva, Pixellab) and edit video clips (CapCut, InShot) to move fast.
